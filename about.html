About Me

This is what I do

I was born in Zimbabwe in 1997 on a farm in Trelawney. I was homeschooled and grew up playing many sports, such as: cricket, rugby, squash, hockey, tennis, table tennis, archery, track & field, et al.

I qualified as an Intermediate Paramedic at ACE Air & Ambulance Pvt Ltd. I worked as a road ambulance medic, and also went on to be an instructor in paramedics and first aid. Finally, I completed my Helicopter & Remote Rescue training and worked as a helicopter medic for bush rescue.

I then came to Bob Jones University to pursue a Pre-Med degree before going on to study surgery. However, last year I realized I would enjoy a career in agriculture more than a career in medicine. So, I switched to major in Zoology & Wildlife Biology as an intermediate step and plan to transfer to agriculture after this semester.
